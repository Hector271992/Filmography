Hector Bosquez... (Photo taken by Isaiah Black)

Hector Bosquez is an American-Hispanic actor known for his roles in Unusual Detectives, Identity: Zero, and Writer's Block. He is best known for his role as David in the action comedy, The Beast from Dark Below (written, directed and produced by Christopher Vanbrussel), along side Sharif Byer, who played his best friend, Mel. He is set to play a young actor named, Steve Almanzar in the upcoming semi-drama film, Rumor. He was also part of short comedic skit entitled Good Cop, Bad Cop, Mom Cop, where he plays the Bad-Ass Detective Mike Kelly. Hector has Directed two films. He made his Co-Directorial debut, alongside Espirito Domingo in, Dark Voice, a psychological suspense about a young man who is being stalked on the phone by an assailant he tormented years earlier. His second Co-Directorial film was, Dream Giant, a silent family-drama about a young man who overcame the odds of being told that he would never amount to anything. Rumor (2019) will be his third Directorial, first single-directorial, first self produced and second writen film currently in filming status, the first being Identity: Zero. Since the age of 5, Hector always dreamed of becoming an actor on television. He admired shows such as Power Rangers, Mystic Knights, Xena: Warrior Princess, starring Lucy Lawless. In his early childhood days, he would often immitate characters from popular tv shows and make up alternate stories to add more conflict. Amongst many tv and movie characters, he mainly admired Xena the warrior princess, the blue Power Panger rangiung from Mighty Morphin to Lightspeed Rescue, and Tarzan. He also had a childhood Obsession with the villain from Power trangers in Space and the Wicked Witch of the West from The Wizard of Oz.

Born Hector Luis Bosquez on February 16, 1992 in Brooklyn, New York, the son of two city workers, Hector always had big dreams of one day being on television. He was born to a Mother and a father, both of Puerto Rican decent. His mother works for the Board of Education as a Teacher's Assistant and his father works as a Cordinator for the Board of Elections. His mother, Rosa, also born in Brooklyn, moved to Puerto Rico as a teenager and lived there till she was 18 years of age then returned to the States where she met his father, Hector Sr. His father came to the states as a young man, where he currently resides to this day. Hector attended P.S. 24 Elementary School where he graduated class of 2004. He went on to attend William Alexander Middle School 51, graduating class of 2007, moving on to George Westinghouse High School, Graduating class of 2011 (2012 with his Diploma) and now is in his final semester at the Borough of Manhattan Community College, where he has been since Spring 2013, with the exception of taking 2 semesters off. "I have been at BMCC for too long now," he quotes, "It's time for me to move on to the next phase in my life where I can continue to find myself and perfect my craft."
